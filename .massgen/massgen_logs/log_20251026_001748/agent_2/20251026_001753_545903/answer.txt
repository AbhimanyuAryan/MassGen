France has had several capitals throughout its history, but by far the most popular and historically significant is **Paris**. While cities like Tours (during World War I and II) and Vichy (under the Vichy regime) served as temporary seats of government, none compare to Paris.

Paris is considered the most popular for several key reasons:

1.  **Duration and Continuity:** Paris has been the capital for the longest continuous period, serving as the primary seat of power for centuries, from the early Capetian kings to the present day.
2.  **Cultural and Economic Hub:** It has consistently been the cultural, economic, and political heart of France, fostering immense artistic, intellectual, and commercial development.
3.  **Iconic Landmarks:** Paris is home to an unparalleled number of world-renowned landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame Cathedral, the Louvre Museum, and the Palace of Versailles (historically the seat of the monarchy near Paris), which contribute to its global recognition and allure.
4.  **Historical Events:** Numerous pivotal historical events, from revolutions to peace treaties, have unfolded in Paris, solidifying its central role in French and European history.
5.  **Global Influence:** Paris has exerted significant global influence in fashion, cuisine, art, and philosophy, making it one of the world's most recognizable and beloved cities.
