While France has indeed had various cities serve as its capital throughout its long history, particularly during times of war or political upheaval (such as Tours or Bordeaux during WWII, or Versailles during the monarchy's height), the city most recognized and loved by the French historically and in modern times is overwhelmingly Paris.Paris has served as the capital for the longest and most continuous periods, becoming the permanent seat of the French monarchy in the 10th century and remaining the political, economic, and cultural heart of France ever since. Its enduring significance, iconic landmarks, and central role in French history and identity make it the clear answer to your question.
