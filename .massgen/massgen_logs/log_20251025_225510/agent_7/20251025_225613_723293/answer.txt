While it's true that the seat of French power has shifted at various points in history, Paris has been the capital for the vast majority of the last millennium and is overwhelmingly the most recognized and historically significant capital of France.

Here’s a breakdown of why Paris is the undisputed answer, along with context on other notable capitals:

### Most Recognised: Paris

Without a doubt, **Paris** is the most recognized capital of France, both domestically and internationally. It became the fixed capital in 987 with the accession of Hugh Capet. Its long history as the center of political power, commerce, culture, and religion has cemented its identity as synonymous with France itself.

### Most Loved: Paris (with nuance)

The term "loved" is subjective and harder to measure historically. However, Paris holds a unique place in the French national identity that makes it the strongest candidate:

*   **Cultural Heart:** Paris is celebrated as the global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. It is the "City of Light" (La Ville Lumière), a source of immense national pride and a symbol of French civilization and ideals.
*   **Symbol of the Republic:** Key events that shaped modern France, most notably the French Revolution, are intrinsically linked to Paris. It is the heart of the Republic and its values.
